About Me

I am an epidemiology graduate with a strong interest in statistical methods, social determinants of health, and infectious diseases. Alongside my academic background, I bring broad career and life experience: editing and writing for leading medical journals, co-authoring books, serving as a school governor, and mentoring others in research communication. I am proficient in R and STATA, enjoy coding, and speak fluent French.

  • Education: MSc Epidemiology (London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine, Merit); BSc Physiology & Psychology (University of Adelaide); Postgraduate Diplomas in Psychology and Science Communication.
  • Editorial Experience: Senior Assistant Editor at The Lancet, technical editor for WHO Bulletin and The Medical Journal of Australia, freelance editor/writer.
  • Other Skills: Data analysis, science communication, teaching, governance, multilingual communication.

About the Course

Generative AI for Work & Research is an 8-week live cohort course by GRAPH Network that equips participants with practical skills in no-code AI tools for task automation, research, and communication. The program combines academic rigor with hands-on workshops, peer collaboration, and a final capstone project.

Key Modules include:

  • Websites, Documents & Presentations with AI
  • AI-Automated Data Analysis
  • Generative AI for Research & Text Analysis
  • Custom GPTs and Chatbots
  • AI for Task Automation
  • AI for Communication & Outreach
  • Capstone Project & Showcase

The course features weekly workshops, live help sessions, personalized feedback, and lifetime access to tutorials—culminating in a project presentation to peers and mentors.
